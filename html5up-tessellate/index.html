Cara's Corner is a dwelling of exposition, story-telling, and creation. From professional projects to quaint stories to symbolic art to knick-knacky creations, it highlights different facets of my life. No person is as flat as as graphene, and it's the joy in discovering one's layers that bring dimension into relationships of all different types, from professional to familial to personal.

About Cara

I am a current sophomore at the University of California, Berkeley studying Electrical Engineering & Computer Science and Molecular & Cell Biology with an emphasis in Neurobiology (or Immunology & Pathogenesis).

Although I did have a tough first year, I grew from first to second semester, and did significantly better in the latter. I'm a fast learner and extremely dedicated and hard worker, with a slight problem refusing any task. Anything that comes under my paradigm is treated with an unwavering dedication and is always completed with precision and attention to detail.

I have a wide array of interests and contrary to popular belief, I do not believe spreading yourself in a variety of directions will diminish passion and dedication to any particular field, so long as you find quality time for each. Computer science is something I picked up very late and I'm still exploring the intricacies and applications of the subject. However, diseases, from neurodegenerative to highly infectious to genetic, has always been a passion of mine, coupled with a deep love of social work. I hope to be able to combine these focuses eventually into a more funneled area of research and application.

As a self-identifying inventor of useful (or not) paraphernalia and creator of nutty ideas, I pride myself in keeping a journal (and in the modern day, Trello) of creations, from web services to apps to chemical and material science ventures to crazy genetic engineering. These creations vary in stages of completion but I can generally categorise them in the following: completed, highly buggy but vaguely done, started-but-now-sitting-in-storage, planned, and pure imagination.

I would personally love to talk to anyone about these ideas, not just for developing them further but also just to talk to people. I enjoy bouncing ideas and discussions off of experts of any subject, so please don't hesitate to contact me if you're interested in sharing thoughts over tea and pastries!

Cara's Portfolio

Photography has been a passion of mine for several years now. I find it's rather beautiful in the way that you can choose how to represent the images and memories you see through multiple techniques and manipulations of scenes. Often I find that the photos I take tend to be of mundane daily bits 'n' bobs, seen from new perspectives, those that embue the subject with vivacity, colour, and life.

Art is something special. The canvas is a rippling fabric, that changes with every brushstroke, every caress. For every piece I put out, I have this ritual: I embed in it a little bit of my breath and a thought or wish, then seal it. To me, each painting is like a vessel of the soul, covered with an image from the mind (or internet), with inspiration from the heart, and a breath from the soul.
